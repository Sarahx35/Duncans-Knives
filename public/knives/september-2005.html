A Plain Steel Sheath Knife

September 2005

I made this knife for a KITH on British Blades. KITH stands for knife in the hat and the idea is that a number of people make a knife of a particular style, and when they are all ready, all the names go in a hat. When the draw is done, each person sends their knife to another person and recieves a random knife in return.

I had to send mine to Dan in Hong Kong, and received a nice knife from Shing in the UK The photos were taken in Hong Kong, because I forgot to take any of the finished knife.

The blade is forged from 3/4 x 1/4 inch O1 steel bar, with a brass guard and a maple handle.
